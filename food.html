FOOD


Before you read, beware: Czech food is delicious, comforting, very filling and addictive, so make sure you reserve enough time to walk off those calories

Vetrnik

Prague loves sweet dishes – particularly pastries. Arguably the city’s finest sweet snack has to be vetrnik. While you’ll find a whole host of places offering this creamy filled choux pastry, you’re likely to find just as many variants – so if you like it, re-discover it all over again at another restaurant or café.

Trdelník (Chimney Cake)

Known by the name ‘chimney cake’, trdelník consists of yeast dough that is rolled out before being wrapped around a wooden or metal stick, which is cooked over, traditionally, an open coal flame. The outside is crispy while the inside, often coated with hazelnut or chocolate (yummy), is lovely and gooey.

Kolache

We can’t round off the dessert list without including kolache. Yes, it’s another pastry – but we can’t help it! These scrumptious dough treats are Czech through and through. Today, they are enjoyed all around the world in different ways. But kolache in Prague is (yes, you guessed it) sweet, not savoury.

Chlebíčky

Also known by the name ‘open-faced sandwich’, chlebíčky is one of the city’s most popular snacks. It consists of a slice of baguette-like bread that is finished off with a variety of toppings – hence why it’s called an open-faced sandwich as no bread is placed on top of the contents.

Schnitzel

A favourite of European cuisine, Prague schnitzel is either breaded pork or chicken that is often served with potato salad or creamy mash. There’s a very good chance you’ll love schnitzel, and like the locals, you’ll be arguing over which variant is best!


Ovocné Knedlíky

One such dish is sweet dumplings stuffed with fruit or jam. Popular fruit fillings include strawberries, blueberries and apricots.Often these dumplings are accompanied alongside sugar, melted butter, cottage cheese and many other toppings or condiments.
